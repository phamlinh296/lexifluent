COLLOCATION EXTRACTION RULES

Extract the 3–5 most IELTS-useful collocations for each vocabulary word.

PRIORITY ORDER:
1. Verb + Noun:        "pose a threat", "draw a conclusion", "play a crucial role"
2. Adjective + Noun:   "significant impact", "adverse effects", "pressing issue"
3. Adverb + Adjective: "deeply concerned", "highly relevant", "widely recognized"
4. Verb + Preposition: "contribute to", "result in", "lead to", "beneficial to"

IELTS TOPIC PRIORITY — prefer collocations used in:
  environment, technology, education, health, crime, globalization, urbanization

FORMAT — return as array of strings, each 2–4 words:
  ["detrimental to", "prove detrimental", "detrimental effects", "environmentally detrimental"]

AVOID:
  - Collocations that are rare, literary, or archaic
  - Full sentences (phrases only)
  - Repetitive patterns across different words
  - Informal collocations (e.g., "dead tired", "cost a fortune")
