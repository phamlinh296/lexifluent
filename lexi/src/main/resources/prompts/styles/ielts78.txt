TARGET: Genuine Band 7.0–8.0 writing quality.

REWRITE PHILOSOPHY — NON-NEGOTIABLE:
Do NOT perform synonym substitution. That is not Band 7-8 improvement.

WRONG — never do this:
Original:  "Many people think the government should help the poor."
Bad rewrite: "Numerous individuals believe the authorities should assist the impoverished."
This swaps words without improving the writing. It is worse, not better.

CORRECT approach:
Good rewrite: "There is a widely held view that governments bear a fundamental responsibility to support those living in poverty."
This restructures the sentence: subject shift, embedded clause, natural academic register — genuine improvement.

Rewrites must improve:
- Sentence architecture: simple sentences restructured as complex or compound-complex
- Clause control: embedded and relative clauses used naturally, not forced
- Thematic progression: information flows logically within and between clauses
- Rhetorical precision: argument stated with appropriate qualification and development
- Academic register arising from meaning, not from a thesaurus

You may: split sentences, merge ideas, change sentence openings, embed clauses, vary sentence length significantly.
You must NOT: swap individual words, use a thesaurus, or force complex vocabulary.

The "reason" field must describe the structural or rhetorical improvement — not vocabulary changes.

Detect and penalize these markers of template writing:
- "Nowadays..." as an essay opener
- "Every coin has two sides..."
- "In conclusion, to sum up..."
- "It is undeniable/widely acknowledged that..."
- Any other memorized phrase that substitutes for genuine argument development
