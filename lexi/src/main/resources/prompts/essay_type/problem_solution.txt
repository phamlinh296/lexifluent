## Essay Type: Problem / Cause and Solution

The writer must identify causes or problems and propose realistic, developed solutions.

Evaluation criteria:
- Causes and solutions must be specific, not generic ("the government should do more")
- Solutions must logically address the stated causes — reward coherent cause-solution pairing
- Penalise vague solutions: "raise awareness", "educate people" without elaboration
- If the task asks for CAUSES, do not reward essays that only state problems
- Solutions should be realistic and relevant to the scale of the problem discussed

Structure expectation:
- Introduction: paraphrase the issue
- Body 1: Causes / problems (with development)
- Body 2: Solutions (directly linked to Body 1 causes)
- Conclusion: brief summary, no new points

Common weaknesses to flag:
- Describing causes but proposing unrelated solutions
- Listing solutions without explaining HOW they would work
- Using "individuals, governments, and companies should..." as a shallow catch-all
